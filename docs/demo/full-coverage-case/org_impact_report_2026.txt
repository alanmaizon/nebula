Eastside Family Bridge
Program Impact Report - 2026

Organization profile:
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit providing housing stabilization and family support across East Portland and Gresham.
- Completed full program cycles in 2024-2025 and 2025-2026.

Need and population served:
- 57% of enrolled households were at risk of eviction within 60 days at intake.
- 74% of households served included at least one child under age 18.
- 49% of participant households reported limited English proficiency.
- 41% of participants missed at least one appointment due to transportation barriers.

Program model in 2026:
- Each enrolled household received weekly case management plus a monthly legal aid clinic referral.
- Case managers used bilingual outreach in English, Spanish, and Russian.
- Transportation vouchers were issued for court dates, school meetings, and medical appointments.
- Food security coordination was delivered through two partner food pantries.

2026 outcomes:
- 390 households enrolled in intensive case management.
- 301 households remained stably housed for at least six months after intake.
- 212 households increased monthly income through benefits enrollment or employment placement.
- 73% of school-age youth in participating households improved attendance by at least five percentage points.

Evaluation approach:
- Baseline assessments completed at intake for all households.
- Follow-up checks at 90 and 180 days recorded housing status, income change, and appointment adherence.
- Monthly QA audits reviewed a 10% sample of case files for data quality and service fidelity.
- Quarterly performance dashboard shared with board and county partners.
